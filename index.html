I am Verity Went

Verity Went is a powerful and compelling speaker whose life changed overnight in a way few people could imagine.

A lifelong migraine sufferer from the age of eight, Verity had learned to live with pain and unpredictability.

What she could never have prepared for was waking up on 4 October 2023 with a completely different accent.

About Verity

Born and raised in the West Midlands, Verity suddenly found herself speaking with a Geordie accent â€” a rare neurological phenomenon later diagnosed as Functional Neurological Disorder (FND) and Foreign Accent Syndrome (FAS).

The experience was not only medically shocking but emotionally devastating, as many people initially believed she was faking it.

Despite public scepticism, online abuse, and misunderstanding, Verity chose to speak out. Following appearances across national TV and radio, including ITV's This Morning and BBC Radio 2's Jeremy Vine Show, she has become an important voice in raising awareness of hidden neurological conditions and the realities of living with an invisible disability.


Speaking & Impact

Verity's story resonates deeply with audiences. She speaks with honesty, warmth, and humor about resilience, identity, judgment, and finding strength when your life takes an unexpected turn.

Her talks are consistently described as moving, eye-opening, and inspirational by attendees who find themselves profoundly moved by her candour and vulnerability.

Demand & Availability

Verity is in high demand across the UK for keynote talks, conferences, community events, health forums, and education settings. She is also a popular guest on podcasts and vodcasts, where her authenticity and openness make for compelling conversation.

In The Media

Verity has appeared on some of the UK's most prominent television and radio shows, sharing her remarkable story with millions of viewers and listeners.

Her appearances have sparked important conversations about neurological conditions, mental health, and resilience in the face of adversity.
